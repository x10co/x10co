Methodology

SPI Ratings

Each team receives an offensive rating (expected goals scored vs an average team on a neutral field) and a defensive rating (expected goals conceded). These combine into an overall Soccer Power Index — the percentage of available points (win=3, draw=1, loss=0) that team would earn against an average opponent over many repeated matches.

Poisson Model

We fit a maximum-likelihood Poisson regression per league to estimate each team's attack and defense parameters. Given two teams, expected goals are computed with a league-specific home advantage. We then:

  • Generate two Poisson distributions around those expected goals
  • Build a full score matrix of all possible results
  • Apply diagonal inflation (~9%) to correct for draw frequency
  • Read off win/draw/loss probabilities

Inspired by FiveThirtyEight

This model is inspired by FiveThirtyEight's Soccer Power Index, originally created by Nate Silver in 2009. Key elements borrowed from their methodology:

  • Offensive & defensive rating separation
  • SPI as % of available points vs average team
  • Poisson process for goal-scoring
  • Draw probability inflation on the diagonal
  • Per-league home advantage estimation

Data Source

Match results from football-data.co.uk covering the 2025–26 season across 22 European leagues including Premier League, La Liga, Bundesliga, Serie A, Ligue 1, and many more. The model uses only final scorelines for rating estimation.

Home Advantage

Each league gets its own home advantage parameter estimated from the data (in log-goals units). Typical values range from ~0.1 to ~0.33. La Liga (0.324) and Premier League (0.198) are estimated from this season's data.

Limitations

  • No xG or shot data used (only scorelines)
  • No transfer market valuations for preseason priors
  • No cross-league strength calibration
  • Ratings are within-league only
  • Early season data has high uncertainty
